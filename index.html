    Frequently Asked Questions?

    This section has common questions that comes in your mind related to IPO.

    How to Subscribe to an IPO?
    +
    • Step 1: Login to your respective service provider.
    • Step 2: Click on the IPO section.
    • Step 3: Select the IPO you want to bid and enter the relevant details.
    • Step 4: Once the form is completed, place you make the payment or give permission.
    Should I buy an IPO first day?
    +

    It depends on various factors including company fundamentals, market conditions, and your investment goals. Consider researching the company thoroughly before making a decision.

    How do you know if an IPO is good?
    +

    Evaluate the company's business model, financial performance, growth prospects, management quality, and market position.

    How to check IPO allotment status?
    +

    You can check your IPO allotment status through the registrar's website, BSE/NSE website, or through your broker's platform using your PAN number and application number.

    What is issue size?
    +

    Issue size refers to the total amount of money a company aims to raise through its IPO. It's calculated by multiplying the number of shares being offered by the price band.

    How many shares in a lot?
    +

    The number of shares in a lot varies for each IPO. It's typically designed to keep the minimum investment amount reasonable, usually between ₹10,000 to ₹15,000.

    How is the lot size calculated?
    +

    Lot size is calculated to ensure the minimum investment remains accessible to retail investors, typically keeping it between ₹10,000-₹15,000 by dividing this amount by the lower price band.

    Who decides the IPO price band?
    +

    The IPO price band is decided by the company in consultation with merchant bankers based on company valuation, market conditions, and peer comparison analysis.

    What is IPO GMP?
    +

    IPO GMP (Grey Market Premium) is the premium at which IPO shares are traded in the unofficial grey market before they are officially listed on the stock exchange.

    How many lots should I apply for IPO?
    +

    For retail investors, you can apply for 1-13 lots. Applying for 1 lot gives you higher chances of allotment due to the lottery system used for oversubscribed IPOs.
